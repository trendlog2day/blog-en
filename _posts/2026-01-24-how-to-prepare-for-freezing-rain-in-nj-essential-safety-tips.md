## Why Freezing Rain is Different

Unlike snow, which piles up, freezing rain looks like normal rain until it hits a surface. Then it turns into a sheet of glaze. I have found that many people underestimate this. It weighs down tree limbs and power lines significantly faster than snow.

Because of this, your preparation needs to focus less on shoveling and more on survival without utilities. The Asbury Park Press noted that freezing rain is a primary driver for major grid failures, so having a plan for when the lights go out is priority number one.

## Know Your Zone: North vs. South Jersey

New Jersey weather is rarely uniform. While the north might get buried in snow, the coastline often faces a different mess. If you are checking the South Jersey snow forecast weekend outlooks, pay attention to the "wintry mix" line. That is usually where the freezing rain bands set up.

Keep an eye on local alerts. When you hear about a Mikie Sherrill state of emergency winter storm NJ declaration, it usually means commercial vehicle restrictions and a plea for regular drivers to stay home. This isn't just about snow accumulation; it is about the inability to stop on icy roads.
